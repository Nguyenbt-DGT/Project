# Kickoff Notes

## 1. Product Vision

Nail down the Product Vision (what does it become 6 months to 1 year from now? what matters at each stage). This is the part that can't be copied — it will be the app's core value => **Make our app the only one!**

## 2. Business Model (How to Make Money)

### 2.1 Subscription (monthly/yearly)

- Decide what's free and what's paid.
- Possible: let users access AI features only with a subscription.

### 2.2 One-time Purchase

- Not a strong preference, since the app needs to wow users.

### 2.3 Third-party Revenue

- No subscription, no purchase — we make money from a third party instead.
- Example: for now it's for our own use, but later if parts shops, repair shops, or riding clubs want to promote something, we could show it on the app and charge them for the promotion (requires a large enough user base).

### 2.4 Open Questions

- What else needs to be done to serve a mass audience?
- Should we consider international markets?
- At each stage, which revenue stream should be primary and which secondary?

## 3. Feature Ideas

- Add Google Reviews (Public API).
- GPS location can also be pulled from the Google API.

### 3.1 Car Tracking (good idea, needs more thought)

1. Car has Apple CarPlay or Android Auto.
2. Upon connecting => the phone recognizes this is your car and can track the exact vehicle you're driving.
3. Could alternatively use an Apple Tag or an accessory we sell bundled with the app.

- Need to list out everything we could realistically track.

## 4. MVP & Execution Strategy

1. Ship the MVP first => get basic features working first.
2. Collect feedback from users => iterate and improve.
3. In the early stage, focus on the human experience.
4. **"Fake It" AI approach**: have AI draft entire touring routes across Vietnam => filter down to the best trips to show customers. Build the feature so people can try it out, then apply AI to make it better => only then think about monetizing it.
